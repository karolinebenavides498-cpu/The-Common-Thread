The Rise of Fast Fashion

Clothing is a part of our everyday lives, but the journey behind each garment is often overlooked. From the resources used to create fabrics to the waste left behind after clothes are discarded, fashion has a much larger impact than what we see in stores.

Fast fashion has made clothing cheaper and easier to access, but it has also increased textile waste, pollution, and the use of natural resources. Understanding these impacts is the first step toward creating a more sustainable future.

Did You Know?

Textile Waste

Millions of tons of clothing are discarded every year, with much of it ending up in landfills instead of being reused or recycled.

Water Usage

Producing clothing requires large amounts of water, especially when growing and processing certain fibers.

Carbon Emissions

Creating and transporting clothing contributes to greenhouse gas emissions that affect our planet.
