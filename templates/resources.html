Resources

Looking to make more sustainable fashion choices? These local businesses, organizations, and educational resources can help you reduce textile waste and learn more about creating a more sustainable future.

Sustainable Fashion in Los Angeles

Goodwill Southern California

Shop for secondhand clothing or donate items you no longer wear. Purchasing pre-owned clothing helps extend the life of garments while supporting local community programs.

Out of the Closet Thrift Stores

A thrift store chain throughout Los Angeles where donated clothing gets a second life while supporting healthcare programs in the community.

SUAY

A Los Angeles company specializing in clothing repair, textile recycling, upcycling, and community dye baths to help keep textiles out of landfills.

The Center for Reuse + Repair

A Los Angeles nonprofit dedicated to repairing, reusing, and redesigning textiles while teaching practical skills to the community.

Organizations & Ways to Help

Garment Worker Center

A Los Angeles organization that advocates for garment workers' rights, fair wages, and safer working conditions throughout the fashion industry.

Fashion Revolution

A global movement encouraging greater transparency and sustainability throughout the fashion industry.

Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)

Learn more about reducing waste, recycling materials, and protecting the environment through sustainable choices.

Ellen MacArthur Foundation

Discover how a circular economy can reduce textile waste through reuse, repair, recycling, and responsible production.
